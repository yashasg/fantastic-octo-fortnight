## Current Situation

The app was previously called **"Eye & Posture Reminder"** — functional but generic. It reads like a feature description, not a brand. In the App Store, it would drown among "Eye Break Timer," "Smart Posture Reminder," and dozens of similar titles. The app has since been renamed to **kshana** (Sanskrit: क्षण, "a moment, an instant").

## Competitive Landscape

Key names already in use in the iOS wellness/break-reminder space:

| Name | Focus | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| LookAway | Eye breaks (Mac-first, iOS companion only) | Strong brand, $19+ paid |
| iCare | Eye break reminders | Free, 20-20-20 focused |
| Eye Care 20 20 20 | Eye breaks | Descriptive, forgettable |
| Posture AI | Posture correction | Camera-based, different approach |
| Stand Up! | Stand/stretch breaks | Movement-focused |
| StretchMinder | Stretching breaks | Exercise-focused |
| Time Out | Screen breaks | General-purpose break timer |
| Standland | Stand reminders | Gamified |

**Our gap:** No iOS app combines eye breaks + posture reminders with screen-time-triggered intelligence and a calming wellness aesthetic. The name should own this space.

## Name Candidates

### 1. Restwell
- **Strategy:** Compound/portmanteau ("rest" + "well/wellness")
- **Why it works:** Directly communicates the app's purpose — resting well during screen time. Warm, approachable, wellness-coded. Aligns with "Restful Grove" design language.
- **App Store searchability:** Distinctive enough to own search results. Would benefit from subtitle containing "eye break & posture reminder."
- **Trademark notes:** No major iOS app uses this name. Low conflict risk.

### 2. Softsight
- **Strategy:** Compound ("soft" + "sight")
- **Why it works:** Evokes gentle, eye-friendly care. "Soft" matches the non-judgmental tone; "sight" anchors it to eye health. Feels premium and intentional.
- **App Store searchability:** Unique, no direct competitors with this name. Strong brand potential.
- **Trademark notes:** No wellness app found under this name. Clean.

### 3. Canopy
- **Strategy:** Nature-inspired / metaphorical
- **Why it works:** A canopy provides shade and shelter — a perfect metaphor for protecting your eyes and body from screen strain. Aligns beautifully with the sage-green grove aesthetic.
- **App Store searchability:** Some apps use "Canopy" (Canopy Care — cancer care; Canopy leadership app) but none in the break-reminder or digital wellness space. Differentiable with subtitle.
- **Trademark notes:** Medium caution — name is used in other app contexts. Would need "Canopy - Screen Wellness" or similar to distinguish.

### 4. Grovemind
- **Strategy:** Compound / nature-inspired ("grove" + "mind")
- **Why it works:** Directly references the Restful Grove design concept. "Mind" signals mindfulness and awareness. Feels like a living, caring companion.
- **App Store searchability:** Highly unique. No competing apps. Easy to own.
- **Trademark notes:** Clean — no known conflicts.

### 5. Respite
- **Strategy:** Evocative / dictionary word
- **Why it works:** Means "a short period of rest or relief" — literally what the app delivers. Elegant, slightly literary, feels premium. Non-clinical.
- **App Store searchability:** No major wellness app uses this name. Clean search results. Would need subtitle for discoverability on eye/posture terms.
- **Trademark notes:** Low risk in iOS wellness space.

### 6. Gentlebreak
- **Strategy:** Compound / descriptive ("gentle" + "break")
- **Why it works:** Communicates the app's non-preachy, calming approach to break reminders. "Gentle" matches the tone perfectly. Immediately understood.
- **App Store searchability:** Contains "break" which helps ASO. Unique compound. No competition.
- **Trademark notes:** Clean.

### 7. Eyase
- **Strategy:** Abstract / invented (portmanteau of "eye" + "ease")
- **Why it works:** Short, memorable, pronounceable (EYE-aze). Contains "eye" for instant association. "Ease" communicates comfort. Feels modern and app-native.
- **App Store searchability:** Completely unique. Risk: users may not know how to spell or search for it.
- **Trademark notes:** No known conflicts. Note: an eyase is also a falconry term (a young hawk taken from the nest), which is nature-adjacent but obscure enough not to cause confusion.

### 8. Dwell
- **Strategy:** Evocative / dictionary word
- **Why it works:** "Dwell" means to live or remain — the app helps you dwell in your body, not just your screen. Short, strong, premium feel.
- **App Store searchability:** No prominent wellness app uses it on iOS. Clean. But very generic as a word — harder to own SEO/ASO.
- **Trademark notes:** Medium caution — "Dwell" is used in architecture/design media. Could cause brand confusion outside the app space.

### 9. Kindscreen
- **Strategy:** Compound / descriptive ("kind" + "screen")
- **Why it works:** Flips "screen time" into something caring. Signals that the app makes your screen experience kinder to your body. Aligns with non-judgmental tone.
- **App Store searchability:** Contains "screen" for ASO. Unique compound. Easy to find.
- **Trademark notes:** Clean — no known conflicts.

### 10. Groverest
- **Strategy:** Compound / nature-inspired ("grove" + "rest")
- **Why it works:** Directly ties to the Restful Grove aesthetic. Evokes resting in a shaded grove. Unique and brandable.
- **App Store searchability:** Completely unique. Would need subtitle for discoverability.
- **Trademark notes:** Clean.

### 11. Blinkmind
- **Strategy:** Compound ("blink" + "mind")
- **Why it works:** "Blink" = eye care. "Mind" = mindfulness/posture awareness. Together they capture both features. Memorable and active-sounding.
- **App Store searchability:** Unique. "Blink" alone is crowded (security cameras), but "Blinkmind" is clean.
- **Trademark notes:** "Blink" as standalone has conflicts (Amazon Blink cameras), but the compound is safe.

### 12. Sageleaf
- **Strategy:** Nature-inspired / abstract
- **Why it works:** "Sage" = wisdom + the app's signature color. "Leaf" = nature, growth, organic wellness. Beautiful brand imagery. Works perfectly with the yin-yang logo in sage/mint.
- **App Store searchability:** Unique. No competition. Would need strong subtitle.
- **Trademark notes:** Clean.

### 13. Easegaze
- **Strategy:** Compound ("ease" + "gaze")
- **Why it works:** "Ease your gaze" — poetic and descriptive. Immediately communicates eye comfort. Flows well phonetically.
- **App Store searchability:** Contains relevant terms. Unique. Would rank for "eye" adjacent searches.
- **Trademark notes:** "Gaze" is used by some apps (video chat), but "Easegaze" is clean.

---

## Top 3 Recommendations

### 🥇 1. Restwell

**Rationale:** The strongest all-around choice. It's immediately understandable ("rest well"), warm, and brandable. It communicates the app's core promise without being clinical. Works beautifully with the Restful Grove aesthetic — it's almost like the aesthetic was designed for this name. Easy to spell, search, and remember. The subtitle "Eye Break & Posture Reminders" handles ASO discoverability.

**Full App Store listing would be:**  
> **Restwell** — Eye Break & Posture Reminders

### 🥈 2. Softsight

**Rationale:** The most premium-feeling option. "Soft" perfectly captures the app's gentle, non-preachy tone, and "sight" anchors it to eye health (the primary feature). It's distinctive, memorable, and completely uncontested in the App Store. Slightly narrower than Restwell — it emphasizes eyes over posture — but the elegance compensates.

**Full App Store listing would be:**  
> **Softsight** — Screen Wellness & Posture Care

### 🥉 3. Respite

**Rationale:** The most sophisticated choice. A real English word meaning exactly what the app delivers — "a short period of rest." It feels premium, calm, and intentional. The risk is that some users may not know the word, but the target audience (knowledge workers, students) will. Clean trademark landscape. Works beautifully as a brand.

**Full App Store listing would be:**  
> **Respite** — Eye & Posture Break Reminders

## ASO (App Store Optimization) Considerations

### Target Keywords
Users search for: `eye break`, `posture reminder`, `screen time health`, `digital wellness`, `20-20-20`, `break timer`, `eye strain`, `posture check`

### Keyword Coverage Strategy

The app name itself should be brandable (not keyword-stuffed). Use the **subtitle** (30 chars max on App Store) and **keyword field** (100 chars) to capture search terms.

| Recommended Name | Subtitle (≤30 chars) | Keyword Field Priorities |
|-------------------|-----------------------|--------------------------|
| Restwell | Eye Break & Posture Reminders | eye strain, screen time, 20-20-20, digital wellness, break timer, posture check, health |
| Softsight | Screen Wellness & Posture Care | eye break, posture reminder, 20-20-20, screen time, break timer, eye strain, digital health |
| Respite | Eye & Posture Break Reminders | screen time, digital wellness, eye strain, 20-20-20, posture check, break timer, health |

### ASO Ranking Analysis

- **"eye break"** — Low-medium competition. iCare and Eye Care 20 20 20 rank. Our subtitle inclusion + keyword field would compete.
- **"posture reminder"** — Medium competition. Posture AI and Smart Posture Reminder rank. Subtitle inclusion helps.
- **"screen time health"** — Low competition. Few apps target this exact phrase. Easy to rank.
- **"digital wellness"** — Low-medium competition. LookAway uses this on macOS. Opportunity on iOS.
- **"20-20-20"** — Medium competition. Multiple eye break apps target this. Include in keywords.

### Key Insight
None of the top competitors combine "eye" + "posture" in their messaging. This is our SEO gap. The subtitle should always contain both "eye" and "posture" to capture cross-category searches that no competitor currently owns.
