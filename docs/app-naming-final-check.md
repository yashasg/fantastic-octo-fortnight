### 5. Cultural Sensitivity

| Aspect | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Primary meaning** | "Sight, vision, gaze" — positive in general usage |
| **Yoga/meditation connection** | Drishti is a specific yoga technique (focused gaze) — positive wellness association ✅ |
| **⚠️ "Evil eye" association** | In South Asian cultures, "drishti" also means **"evil eye" / "nazar"** — someone's envious or harmful gaze that brings bad luck. "Drishti lag gaya" means the evil eye has fallen on you. Rituals to "remove drishti" are common in Indian households. |
| **Religious significance** | Used in Hindu worship practices; "drishti" of a deity is considered sacred |
| **Other languages** | No negative connotations outside South Asian context |

**Verdict: ⚠️ CAUTION** — The "evil eye" association is a real concern. While tech-savvy users will understand the "vision/gaze" meaning, some South Asian users may find the name off-putting for a health app — as if the app is casting a harmful gaze. This cuts both ways: the yoga/meditation community will love it, but the superstitious connotation exists.
