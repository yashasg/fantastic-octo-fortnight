# Sanskrit Name Candidates

**Author:** Roman (Market Researcher)  
**Date:** 2026-07-17  
**Status:** Draft for team review  
**Context:** Yashas requested single-word names from Sanskrit roots, building on the yin-yang / Eastern philosophy aesthetic of the app.

---

## Research Approach

Explored Sanskrit vocabulary across: rest, sight, eyes, vision, breath (prāṇa), pause, calm, balance, care, healing, wellness, harmony, mindfulness, awareness, posture, body, stillness, peace, clarity. Also sourced from Yoga and Ayurveda traditions. Each candidate was checked against live App Store listings via web search. Names from previous rounds (`docs/app-naming-research.md` and `docs/app-naming-classical.md`) are excluded.

**Key filter applied:** Pronunciation ease for Western audiences — this is an App Store name, not an academic paper. Sanskrit has consonant clusters (kṣ, sth, dhr) that English speakers struggle with. I've prioritized words that flow naturally in English while still being authentically Sanskrit.

---

## Candidates

### 1. Samata (समता)
- **Meaning:** Balance, equanimity, evenness, equality of mind
- **Why it fits:** The yin-yang logo is literally about balance — Samata is the Sanskrit word for that concept. It captures the app's core mission: restoring equilibrium between screen work and bodily wellness. In yoga philosophy, samata is the state of being undisturbed and centered. Smooth, three-syllable flow that sounds warm and approachable.
- **Pronunciation:** sah-MAH-tah
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Easy ✅
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app found with this name.

### 2. Netra (नेत्र)
- **Meaning:** Eye; also "leader" or "guide" (one who leads/shows the way)
- **Why it fits:** The literal Sanskrit word for "eye" — the primary function this app protects. The secondary meaning "guide" is a beautiful bonus: Netra guides you to take breaks, protecting your vision. Short, punchy, easy to spell. Two syllables, memorable.
- **Pronunciation:** NEH-trah
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Easy ✅
- **App Store:** ⚠️ **LOW RISK** — MIT's "NETRA" was a research eye-exam prototype (not a consumer App Store listing). "Netra Smart" is a document scanner. "MTG Netra" is an education app. No wellness/break-reminder app uses this name.

### 3. Taraka (तारका)
- **Meaning:** Star; pupil of the eye; that which helps cross over (from darkness to light)
- **Why it fits:** A poetic triple meaning — the pupil of the eye (eye care), a star (guiding light), and "one who helps you cross" (the app helps you transition from screen strain to rest). The word feels luminous and hopeful. Melodic three-syllable flow.
- **Pronunciation:** TAH-rah-kah
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Easy ✅
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app found with this name.

### 4. Achala (अचल)
- **Meaning:** The immovable; stillness; steadfast; mountain
- **Why it fits:** In Sanskrit, Achala means "that which does not move" — perfect for a posture reminder. Mountains are the ultimate symbol of stillness and groundedness. The Buddhist deity Acala (same word) represents unwavering inner calm. Clean and strong-sounding.
- **Pronunciation:** ah-CHAH-lah
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Easy ✅
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app found with this name. The Buddhist deity Acala has cultural presence but no app conflict.

### 5. Sthira (स्थिर)
- **Meaning:** Steady, stable, firm, unwavering
- **Why it fits:** One of the most important words in yoga: "sthira sukham āsanam" (Yoga Sutra 2.46) — a posture should be steady and comfortable. This word literally bridges posture and calm. It's the philosophical foundation of what this app does: help you find steadiness.
- **Pronunciation:** STIH-rah (rhymes with "tiara" without the opening vowel)
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Medium ⚡ — The "sth" cluster is unusual in English, but the word is short enough to learn quickly.
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app found with this name.

### 6. Vishranti (विश्रान्ति)
- **Meaning:** Rest, relaxation, repose, peace
- **Why it fits:** The direct Sanskrit equivalent of "rest" — what the app delivers. In Ayurveda, vishranti is the essential counterbalance to exertion. Beautiful word with a flowing, meditative quality.
- **Pronunciation:** vish-RAHN-tee
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Medium ⚡ — Three syllables, unfamiliar to most English speakers, but phonetically regular once heard.
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app found with this name.

### 7. Drishti (दृष्टि)
- **Meaning:** Sight, vision, gaze, point of focus
- **Why it fits:** In yoga, drishti is the focused gaze point during practice — training the eyes to look with intention rather than strain. This maps perfectly to the 20-20-20 rule: redirect your drishti to rest your eyes. Already recognized in Western yoga culture, which gives it instant credibility.
- **Pronunciation:** DRISH-tee
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Medium ⚡ — The "dr" opening is natural in English, and yoga practitioners already know this word. The "sh" sound flows easily.
- **App Store:** ⚠️ **LOW RISK** — "Drishti" apps exist but are a news aggregator (F1Soft) and an exam prep platform (Drishti IAS). No wellness or break-reminder app uses this name standalone.

### 8. Mudra (मुद्रा)
- **Meaning:** Seal, gesture, symbolic hand position in yoga/meditation
- **Why it fits:** Mudras are the body's language of intention — a physical gesture that creates mental stillness. The app's reminders are like digital mudras: small physical adjustments (look away, straighten up) that shift your state. Already widely known in Western wellness culture.
- **Pronunciation:** MOO-drah
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Easy ✅ — Well-established in Western yoga vocabulary.
- **App Store:** ⚠️ **LOW RISK** — No standalone app called "Mudra" exists. Related apps use it as a compound: "Mudra Magic," "Mudras [YOGA]," "Daily Mudras" — all focused on hand gesture instruction, not break reminders.

### 9. Nivritti (निवृत्ति)
- **Meaning:** Withdrawal, turning inward, cessation, rest from worldly activity
- **Why it fits:** In Hindu philosophy, nivritti is the path of turning inward — withdrawing from external stimulation to find peace. This is exactly what a screen break is: a nivritti from digital stimulation. The concept is profound without being heavy.
- **Pronunciation:** niv-RIT-tee
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Medium ⚡ — Three syllables, phonetically regular. The double "t" is clear.
- **App Store:** ⚠️ **LOW RISK** — "Nivritti" by Paytring is a fintech/payments app. Completely different category with zero audience overlap. "Nivritti Gurukul" is an educational portal. No wellness conflict.

### 10. Rupa (रूप)
- **Meaning:** Form, appearance, beauty; that which is seen
- **Why it fits:** In Buddhist philosophy, rupa is the physical form — the body and its senses, including sight. The app cares for your rupa: your physical body (posture) and your vision (eyes). Short, soft, and unmistakably warm. Two syllables, four letters.
- **Pronunciation:** ROO-pah
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Easy ✅
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS wellness app found with this name.

### 11. Sthiti (स्थिति)
- **Meaning:** State, posture, standing position, stability, maintenance
- **Why it fits:** The Sanskrit word for "posture" and "state of being" — covering both the physical posture reminders and the mental state the app cultivates. In yoga, sthiti is the sustained holding of a pose. Distinctive and specific.
- **Pronunciation:** STIH-tee
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Medium ⚡ — Same "sth" cluster challenge as Sthira, but even shorter.
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app found with this name.

### 12. Kshana (क्षण)
- **Meaning:** A moment, an instant, a flash of time
- **Why it fits:** The app delivers kshanas — brief moments of rest that make all the difference. In Buddhist philosophy, kshana is the smallest unit of time, and awareness of each moment is the foundation of mindfulness. Philosophically perfect.
- **Pronunciation:** KSHAH-nah (the "ksh" sounds like the "x" in "taxi" + "ah")
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Hard ⛔ — The "ksh" consonant cluster has no English equivalent. Most speakers would say "SHAH-nah" (dropping the k), which changes the word.
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app. "Kshana" exists only as a luxury physical magazine service (kshana.life), no App Store presence.

### 13. Shanti (शान्ति)
- **Meaning:** Peace, calm, tranquility, inner stillness
- **Why it fits:** One of the most recognized Sanskrit words worldwide. "Om Shanti" is the universal peace invocation. The app's mission is to restore shanti during screen time. Instantly communicative, warm, and universally positive.
- **Pronunciation:** SHAHN-tee
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Easy ✅ — Already part of Western cultural vocabulary through yoga and meditation.
- **App Store:** ❌ **CONFLICT** — "Shanti - Meditation Timer" by Artem Umanets exists on iOS. Direct overlap in wellness/mindfulness space. Creates brand confusion.

### 14. Prana (प्राण)
- **Meaning:** Breath, life force, vital energy
- **Why it fits:** Prana is the most fundamental concept in yoga — the breath that sustains life. The app's break reminders are moments to restore prana: breathe, look away, realign. Already deeply embedded in Western wellness culture.
- **Pronunciation:** PRAH-nah
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Easy ✅ — Widely known through yoga culture.
- **App Store:** ❌ **CONFLICT** — Multiple apps: "Prana Breath: Calm & Meditate" (4.7★), "Prana Tech" (wearable + breathing + posture!), "Breathwork Prana: Mindfulness," "Prana Wellness AI." Heavily contested name. Prana Tech is especially problematic — it's a breathing + posture training app with a wearable device.

### 15. Dhyana (ध्यान)
- **Meaning:** Meditation, contemplation, focused attention, absorption
- **Why it fits:** Dhyana is the root of the Chinese "Chan" and Japanese "Zen" — one of the most influential Sanskrit words in world culture. Represents deep, focused awareness. The app helps users practice micro-dhyana: brief moments of conscious presence.
- **Pronunciation:** DHYAH-nah (the "dh" is a breathy "d")
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Medium ⚡ — The "dhy" opening is unfamiliar. Most would say "dee-AH-nah," which works acceptably.
- **App Store:** ❌ **CONFLICT** — "Dhyana Wellness Tracker" by AVANTARI exists on iOS. Full wellness tracker with meditation, stress monitoring, sleep tracking. Direct wellness category overlap.

### 16. Nidra (निद्रा)
- **Meaning:** Sleep, rest, deep relaxation
- **Why it fits:** "Yoga Nidra" (yogic sleep) is a well-known relaxation practice — a guided descent into deep rest while remaining conscious. The app's break reminders are mini-nidras: brief, restorative pauses. Recognized in Western yoga.
- **Pronunciation:** NID-rah
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Easy ✅ — Short, clean, familiar through yoga nidra.
- **App Store:** ❌ **CONFLICT** — "Nidra - Sleep & Meditation" by Conviction Labs exists on iOS. Guided Yoga Nidra sessions. Same space and audience.

### 17. Saumya (सौम्य)
- **Meaning:** Gentle, mild, soft, moon-like, pleasing
- **Why it fits:** The Sanskrit word for "gentle" — the defining personality of this app. "Saumya" literally means "having the quality of the moon (Soma)" — cool, calming, and softly luminous. This connects beautifully to the yin-yang aesthetic (moon energy = yin).
- **Pronunciation:** SOW-myah (rhymes with "now" + "myah")
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Medium ⚡ — The diphthong "au" (sounds like "ow") may trip some speakers, but it's two syllables and learnable.
- **App Store:** ⚠️ **CAUTION** — "Face, Body Yoga Studio: Saumya" by Pravin Gondaliya uses "Saumya" as a subtitle. It's a yoga workout app. Different function but same wellness space. The name appears as a subtitle, not the primary brand, which reduces confusion somewhat.

### 18. Viraam (विराम)
- **Meaning:** Pause, stop, rest, cessation
- **Why it fits:** The most literal match: Viraam means "pause" — exactly what the app delivers. In Hindi (derived from Sanskrit), viraam is also the word for the full stop punctuation mark — a beautiful metaphor for the punctuation of rest in your workday.
- **Pronunciation:** vih-RAHM
- **Ease for Western audiences:** Easy ✅ — Two syllables, phonetically clear.
- **App Store:** ❌ **CONFLICT** — "Viraam: Gentle Break Reminder" exists on the **Mac App Store** (by Viraam.app). It's a break reminder with 20-20-20 rule, posture reminders, and Pomodoro timer. **This is a near-identical competitor.** Even though it's Mac-only currently, the overlap is too direct.

## Top 5 Recommendations

### 🥇 1. Samata

**"Balance, restored."**

The strongest overall candidate. Samata means *balance* and *equanimity* — the yin-yang concept expressed in Sanskrit. This word ties the app's visual identity (yin-yang logo) directly to its name with cultural authenticity. It's easy to pronounce (sah-MAH-tah), has zero App Store conflicts, and sounds warm and approachable. English speakers will intuit something calm and centered from the sound alone. The word has depth — in yoga philosophy, samata is the state of mental equilibrium where one is neither elated nor disturbed. That's exactly the state the app helps restore.

> **Samata** — Eye Break & Posture Reminders

### 🥈 2. Netra

**"Your eye's guardian."**

The literal Sanskrit word for "eye" — direct, specific, and unmistakable. Two syllables, five letters, extremely easy to pronounce (NEH-trah). The secondary meaning "guide" adds brand depth: Netra doesn't just care for your eyes, it guides you toward better habits. The App Store landscape has no wellness competition under this name. The MIT "NETRA" project (an eye-exam prototype) actually reinforces the eye-health association without creating confusion — it's a research tool, not a consumer app.

> **Netra** — Screen Break & Posture Care

### 🥉 3. Taraka

**"Light for your eyes."**

A stunning triple meaning: *pupil of the eye*, *star*, and *that which helps cross over* (from strain to rest, from darkness to light). The word connects directly to eye care while carrying a sense of hope and guidance. Three syllables that flow easily (TAH-rah-kah) — no difficult consonant clusters. Completely clean App Store. The "star" association also offers beautiful visual branding possibilities alongside the yin-yang.

> **Taraka** — Eye & Posture Wellness

### 4. Achala

**"Steady as a mountain."**

Sanskrit for "the immovable" — a powerful posture word. In Buddhist tradition, Achala represents unwavering calm amid chaos, which maps perfectly to maintaining composure during long screen sessions. Easy to pronounce (ah-CHAH-lah), no App Store conflicts, and it sounds strong yet peaceful. Best choice if the team wants to emphasize the posture/stillness aspect over eye care.

> **Achala** — Gentle Eye & Posture Breaks

### 5. Drishti

**"Where you focus your gaze."**

The yoga term for "focused gaze point" — already recognized by Western yoga practitioners. Drishti is the most immediately meaningful name for eye care, since it literally means "sight" and "where you look." The App Store has no wellness competition (existing Drishti apps are a news aggregator and exam prep platform). The word has built-in cultural credibility. Medium pronunciation but familiar enough through yoga culture that the target audience will likely know it.

> **Drishti** — Eye Break & Posture Reminders

| Memorability | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| Brandability | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| Pronunciation | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| Aesthetic fit | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |

| App Store clarity | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |

**My take:** If the yin-yang logo and Eastern philosophy aesthetic are central to the brand identity (and they are), a Sanskrit name creates stronger brand coherence than Latin or English. Samata, Netra, and Taraka all pair beautifully with the sage-green, yin-yang visual language. Latin names like Lenis are excellent standalone brands, but Sanskrit names tell a unified story with the existing design direction.

---

## Caution: The "Viraam" Problem

Worth flagging: **Viraam** (विराम, "pause") would have been the single best Sanskrit name for this app — it literally means "pause/rest," it's easy to pronounce, and the meaning is perfect. However, "Viraam: Gentle Break Reminder" already exists on the Mac App Store as a break reminder app with 20-20-20 rule support, posture reminders, and Pomodoro timer. It's essentially the same product concept on a different platform. This is a hard avoid.
