# Classical Language Name Candidates

**Author:** Roman (Market Researcher)  
**Date:** 2026-07-16  
**Status:** Draft for team review  
**Context:** Yashas requested single-word names from Greek, Latin, and Roman roots after "Respite" was found to have App Store conflicts.

---

## Research Approach

Explored Greek, Latin, and Roman cultural vocabulary across themes of: rest, sight, eyes, breath, pause, calm, balance, care, healing, wellness, harmony, and guardianship. Each candidate was checked against live App Store listings via web search. Names from the previous `docs/app-naming-research.md` round are excluded.

---

## Candidates

### 1. Galene
- **Origin:** Greek (Γαλήνη)
- **Meaning:** Calm, serenity, stillness of the sea
- **Why it fits:** Galene was the Greek goddess of calm seas — a perfect metaphor for the app's mission of restoring calm during screen storms. The soft "g" and flowing syllables match the Restful Grove aesthetic. Evokes nature without being literal.
- **Pronunciation:** guh-LEE-nee
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app uses this name. A web-based videoconferencing server called "Galène" exists but has no App Store presence.
- **Trademark risk:** Low — mythological name, no wellness/health conflicts found.

### 2. Lenis
- **Origin:** Latin
- **Meaning:** Gentle, soft, mild, smooth
- **Why it fits:** Literally means "gentle" — the defining personality trait of this app. Short, elegant, easy to spell. Works beautifully as a brand: "Lenis reminds you to look away." The Latin root connects to English "lenient" and "lenity."
- **Pronunciation:** LEH-nis (or LEE-nis)
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app named "Lenis." A developer called "Lenis Srl" publishes unrelated apps, but the standalone name is unclaimed.
- **Trademark risk:** Low — not a common brand term.

### 3. Requies
- **Origin:** Latin
- **Meaning:** Rest, repose, peace, relaxation
- **Why it fits:** The Latin word for rest itself. Root of "requiem" (rest for the dead) but without the somber connotation — requies simply means living rest. Sophisticated, literary, and phonetically beautiful. Carries the same premium energy as "Respite" but with zero App Store conflicts.
- **Pronunciation:** REH-kwee-ess
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app found with this name.
- **Trademark risk:** Very low — obscure Latin word, unlikely to conflict.

### 4. Levamen
- **Origin:** Latin
- **Meaning:** Relief, comfort, solace, alleviation
- **Why it fits:** Communicates exactly what the app delivers — relief from screen strain. The word feels medical-adjacent without being clinical. Root of "alleviate." Has a warm, reassuring sound.
- **Pronunciation:** leh-VAH-men
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app found with this name.
- **Trademark risk:** Very low — extremely obscure.

### 5. Placida
- **Origin:** Latin
- **Meaning:** Peaceful, calm, tranquil, serene
- **Why it fits:** The feminine form of "placidus" (placid). Evokes peaceful waters, calm spaces, quiet moments. The "-a" ending gives it a soft, approachable feel. Works with the sage-green palette — "Placida" sounds like a place you'd want to rest.
- **Pronunciation:** plah-SEE-dah (or PLASS-ih-dah)
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app found with this name.
- **Trademark risk:** Low — dictionary-adjacent word, but distinctive enough to brand.

### 6. Remissio
- **Origin:** Latin
- **Meaning:** Release, relaxation, letting go, slackening
- **Why it fits:** Captures the "let go of tension" message. Root of English "remission" (relief from symptoms). The app is literally a remissio — a release from screen strain. Has an elegant, musical quality.
- **Pronunciation:** reh-MISS-ee-oh
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app found with this name.
- **Trademark risk:** Very low — obscure Latin, unlikely conflicts.

### 7. Lenitas
- **Origin:** Latin
- **Meaning:** Gentleness, softness, mildness, smoothness
- **Why it fits:** The abstract noun form of "lenis" — embodying gentleness as a concept. If Lenis is the adjective, Lenitas is the philosophy. Distinctive, brandable, and unmistakably wellness-coded.
- **Pronunciation:** LEH-nih-tahs
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app found with this name.
- **Trademark risk:** Very low.

### 8. Custos
- **Origin:** Latin
- **Meaning:** Guardian, watcher, protector, keeper
- **Why it fits:** Positions the app as a guardian of your wellbeing — watching over your screen time, protecting your eyes and posture. The "silent protector" angle aligns with the non-judgmental tone. Root of English "custody" and "custodian."
- **Pronunciation:** KOOS-tohs
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app found with this name.
- **Trademark risk:** Low — used in some security/legal contexts but not in wellness apps.

### 9. Ocella
- **Origin:** Latin
- **Meaning:** "Little eye" — diminutive of *oculus* (eye)
- **Why it fits:** A poetic, endearing word for "little eye." Directly anchors the app to eye care while sounding warm and caring — not clinical. The diminutive form adds tenderness that matches the gentle tone. Root of English "ocular."
- **Pronunciation:** oh-CHEL-lah (classical) or oh-SEL-lah (ecclesiastical)
- **App Store:** ✅ **CLEAN** — No iOS app found with this name. Note: "Ocella" is a generic birth control brand name (pharma/medical), but this is a completely different industry with no App Store overlap.
- **Trademark risk:** Medium-low — the pharma brand exists but is Class 5 (pharmaceuticals), not Class 9 (software). Differentiable but worth attorney review.

### 10. Halcyon
- **Origin:** Greek via Latin (ἀλκυών → halcyon)
- **Meaning:** Calm, peaceful, tranquil; relating to the mythical halcyon bird that calmed the seas
- **Why it fits:** One of the most beautiful words in the classical vocabulary. "Halcyon days" = a golden period of peace and calm. The mythology (a bird that brought stillness to winter seas) maps perfectly to an app that brings calm to chaotic screen time.
- **Pronunciation:** HAL-see-on
- **App Store:** ⚠️ **CAUTION** — "Halcyon App" exists (ambient sounds/nature soundscapes by Uwe Horn). Different function but overlapping wellness space. "Halcyon 6" (game) may have been removed.
- **Trademark risk:** Medium — the ambient sounds app creates some confusion in wellness category. Usable with strong subtitle differentiation.

### 11. Quies
- **Origin:** Latin
- **Meaning:** Rest, quiet, repose, stillness, peace
- **Why it fits:** The Latin root of English "quiet." Short, powerful, and directly communicative. Five letters, one syllable of meaning. The word itself feels like what it describes — still and certain.
- **Pronunciation:** KWEE-ays (classical) or KWEE-ess
- **App Store:** ⚠️ **CAUTION** — "Quies: Calm & Wallpapers" exists (daily quotes + calming images by Barachiel Adigbli). Small app with minimal ratings. Overlapping wellness space but different function.
- **Trademark risk:** Medium — same-category conflict. Mitigable with subtitle but creates brand confusion risk similar to Respite.

### 12. Pausa
- **Origin:** Latin (from Greek παῦσις)
- **Meaning:** Pause, stop, cessation, break
- **Why it fits:** Literally "pause" in Latin — the most direct description of what the app does. Instantly understood across Romance languages (Spanish, Italian, Portuguese all use "pausa"). Short and memorable.
- **Pronunciation:** POW-sah
- **App Store:** ⚠️ **CAUTION** — "Pausa - AI Journaling & To-Dos" exists (by Pausai LLC). Different function (journaling) but in the productivity/wellness space.
- **Trademark risk:** Medium — the journaling app is established and well-reviewed.

### 13. Sopor
- **Origin:** Latin
- **Meaning:** Deep sleep, drowsiness, restful slumber
- **Why it fits:** Evokes deep restfulness. Root of English "soporific." Has a drowsy, calming sound quality. Short and distinctive.
- **Pronunciation:** SOH-por
- **App Store:** ⚠️ **LOW RISK** — An old 2013 game called "SOPOR" existed but appears to have been removed. No current active listing found.
- **Trademark risk:** Low — likely clear but verify current App Store status directly.

### 14. Quietus
- **Origin:** Latin
- **Meaning:** Rest, repose, discharge; "being at quiet"
- **Why it fits:** The state of being at rest. More dynamic than "Quies" — suggests arriving at peace, not just static calm. Root of the English adjective "quiet." Has a definitive, confident sound.
- **Pronunciation:** kwee-AY-tus
- **App Store:** ⚠️ **LOW RISK** — "Quietus - Bike" exists (cycling community app by Markus Dirr). Completely different category (sports). Minimal confusion risk.
- **Trademark risk:** Low — the bike app is in a wholly different space.

### 15. Visus
- **Origin:** Latin
- **Meaning:** Sight, vision, the act of seeing
- **Why it fits:** The Latin word for sight/vision itself. Directly anchors the app to eye care. Short, strong, and distinctive. Root of English "visual," "vision," "visor."
- **Pronunciation:** VEE-soos (classical) or VIH-sus
- **App Store:** ⚠️ **CAUTION** — "Vision Board: Visus" exists (vision board app by HAPPUS LLC). Different function but shares the name in its title.
- **Trademark risk:** Medium — the vision board app owns "Visus" as a subtitle. Could cause search confusion.

### 16. Opsin
- **Origin:** Greek (ὄψις, opsis = sight) + scientific suffix
- **Meaning:** Light-sensitive protein in the eye's retina; derived from "opsis" (sight)
- **Why it fits:** A deep-cut scientific reference to the eye's own biology. For the target audience (knowledge workers, STEM-adjacent), this is a clever, insider reference. Short, modern-sounding, and eye-specific.
- **Pronunciation:** OP-sin
- **App Store:** ⚠️ **CAUTION** — "Opsin" is used by SIONYX for their night vision camera hardware companion app. Different category (hardware/cameras) but the eye/vision association overlaps conceptually.
- **Trademark risk:** Medium — SIONYX has an established brand with "Opsin" in the optics space.

### 17. Otium
- **Origin:** Latin
- **Meaning:** Leisure, ease, rest, free time; the Roman ideal of productive leisure (opposite of *negotium*, business)
- **Why it fits:** In Roman culture, *otium* was the highest aspiration — purposeful rest that restored the mind. This is philosophically perfect for an app about intentional breaks. Beautiful word, rich cultural meaning.
- **Pronunciation:** OH-tee-um
- **App Store:** ❌ **CONFLICT** — "Otium: Block Apps Easily" exists (screen time management by Philipp Schmid). **Direct competitor** in digital wellness. Also "Otium Word" puzzle game exists.
- **Trademark risk:** High — same-category competitor. **Not recommended despite excellent name.**

### 18. Ataraxia
- **Origin:** Greek (ἀταραξία)
- **Meaning:** Tranquility, equanimity, freedom from mental disturbance
- **Why it fits:** A cornerstone concept of Stoic and Epicurean philosophy — the state of serene calmness the app aims to restore. Intellectually rich, highly brandable, and resonant with the target audience. Four syllables but flows beautifully.
- **Pronunciation:** at-ah-RAK-see-ah
- **App Store:** ⚠️ **CAUTION** — "Ataraxia - Stoic Journal" exists (BigBalli Consulting LLC). Wellness/philosophy space but different function (journaling vs. break reminders).
- **Trademark risk:** Medium — same philosophical space. The journal app is small but the overlap in tone and audience creates confusion risk.

## Top 5 Recommendations

### 🥇 1. Lenis

**"The gentle guardian of your screen time."**

The strongest overall candidate. "Lenis" means *gentle* in Latin — the single word that best defines this app's personality. It's short (5 letters), easy to spell, easy to pronounce, and instantly brandable. No App Store conflicts whatsoever. The connection to English "lenient" and "lenity" gives English speakers an intuitive sense of its meaning without needing a translation. Works beautifully with the Restful Grove aesthetic — sage green, soft tones, yin-yang balance. The subtitle "Eye Break & Posture Reminders" would handle ASO.

> **Lenis** — Eye Break & Posture Reminders

### 🥈 2. Requies

**"Your moment of rest."**

The Latin word for rest itself — directly communicating what the app delivers, just like "Respite" did, but with zero App Store conflicts. More sophisticated than an English word, which gives it premium brand energy. The connection to "requiem" adds gravitas without heaviness (requies is rest for the living, not the dead). Phonetically beautiful. Completely unclaimed.

> **Requies** — Screen Wellness & Posture Care

### 🥉 3. Galene

**"Calm in the storm of screen time."**

Galene was the Greek goddess of calm seas — a stunning metaphor for an app that brings tranquility to the chaos of extended screen use. The mythology is accessible and beautiful without being obvious. The name sounds like what it means — flowing, gentle, serene. Pairs perfectly with the sage/mint color palette (sea foam, calm waters). No App Store competition.

> **Galene** — Eye & Posture Break Reminders

### 4. Placida

**"A peaceful place for your eyes."**

Latin for "peaceful" — familiar enough through English "placid" that users will intuit its meaning, but distinctive enough as a standalone name. The feminine form gives it warmth and approachability. Sounds like a place name (a grove, a haven, a sanctuary), which maps directly to the "Restful Grove" design concept. Completely unclaimed.

> **Placida** — Gentle Eye & Posture Breaks

### 5. Levamen

**"Relief, delivered gently."**

Latin for "relief" and "comfort." The most descriptive of the clean candidates — it tells you exactly what the app provides. Has a medicinal weight without being clinical. The connection to "alleviate" gives English speakers a foothold. Distinctive, impossible to confuse with other brands, and phonetically warm. Best choice if the team wants to emphasize the app's therapeutic benefit.

> **Levamen** — Eye Break & Posture Reminders
