# Synonym Translations: App Name Candidates

**Author:** Roman (Market Researcher)  
**Date:** 2026-07-18  
**Status:** Draft for team review  
**Context:** Yashas requested single-word translations of 14 English concepts across Greek, Latin, and Sanskrit. Current favorites are **Ocella** (Latin, "little eye") and **Restwell** (English compound). This round explores specific semantic territory not covered in previous naming rounds.

---

## Research Approach

Translated 14 English words (Blink, Posture, Gaze, Glance, Rest, Ease, Breath, Gentle, Watch, Guard, Wink, Stretch, Soothe, Focus) across Greek, Latin, and Sanskrit. Each candidate was filtered against:
- **Pronunciation:** Must be ≤4 syllables, pronounceable for English speakers
- **Duplicates:** Excluded all words from previous rounds (`app-naming-research.md`, `app-naming-classical.md`, `app-naming-sanskrit.md`)
- **App Store:** Checked each candidate against live iOS App Store listings
- **Brandability:** Evaluated as a standalone app name

## 🏆 FINAL TOP 10

### 🥇 1. Anesis (Greek) — "Relief, relaxation"

**The standout discovery of this round.**

Anesis (ἄνεσις) means "loosening, relief, relaxation" — the literal easing of tension. The word captures both the physical (muscle relaxation, eye relief) and emotional (letting go of screen strain) dimensions of what the app does. Three syllables that flow like water: AH-neh-sis. Completely clean App Store. No trademark conflicts.

The connection to English is organic — "anesis" sounds like "at ease." The Greek root (ἀνίημι, "to release") gives it philosophical depth without pretension. This is the kind of word that sounds premium on first hearing and gets more meaningful the more you know.

> **Anesis** — Eye Break & Posture Reminders

**Pairs with Ocella:** Beautifully. Greek + Latin, both classical, both 3 syllables, both elegant. Anesis is the philosophy ("relief"), Ocella is the subject ("little eye").  
**Pairs with Restwell:** Good contrast. Anesis is the premium/classical alternative to Restwell's warm accessibility.

---

### 🥈 2. Nimesha (Sanskrit) — "Blink, twinkling of an eye, a moment"

**The most poetic name in the entire four-round search.**

Nimesha (निमेष) captures the app's essence in a single Sanskrit word: a blink — the tiniest pause — that changes everything. The double meaning is perfect: "blink" (eye care) + "a moment" (the brief break the app delivers). Three syllables that sound like a gentle bell: nee-MAY-shah.

Completely clean App Store. Easy for English speakers despite being Sanskrit. The word has a natural warmth and music that would make it unforgettable as a brand.

> **Nimesha** — Screen Break & Posture Care

**Pairs with Ocella:** Excellent pair. Both reference the eye through different cultural lenses — Latin diminutive vs. Sanskrit metaphor. Nimesha has stronger Eastern/yin-yang resonance.  
**Pairs with Restwell:** Good alternative. Nimesha is the poetic choice; Restwell is the accessible choice.

---

### 🥉 3. Mollis (Latin) — "Soft, tender, gentle"

**The softest-sounding name in four rounds of research.**

Mollis literally means "soft" — and it *sounds* soft. The double-L creates a gentle, rolling quality that mirrors the app's personality. Two syllables, six letters, instantly pronounceable. Root of English "mollify" (to soften, to soothe) and "emollient" (softening agent). Zero App Store conflicts.

This is the word you'd use in Latin poetry to describe a gentle touch, soft light, or tender care. It positions the app as fundamentally soft-natured — never harsh, never naggy.

> **Mollis** — Gentle Eye & Posture Breaks

**Pairs with Ocella:** Perfect Latin siblings. Ocella (little eye) + Mollis (soft) — they share linguistic DNA.  
**Pairs with Restwell:** Different energy. Mollis is more premium/classical; Restwell is more accessible.

---

### 4. Tener (Latin) — "Tender, gentle, delicate"

**Instantly understood, instantly warm.**

Everyone knows what "tender" means — and Tener is its Latin root. Two syllables (TEH-ner), five letters, zero learning curve. The word communicates care and gentleness without needing a translation guide. Clean App Store. The sound is warm, almost like a person's name.

> **Tener** — Eye Break & Posture Reminders

**Pairs with Ocella:** Strong Latin pair. Tener Ocella could even be a tagline ("tender little eye").  
**Pairs with Restwell:** Similar warmth level. Both are immediately understood. Tener has more character.

---

### 5. Komala (Sanskrit) — "Soft, tender, delicate, beautiful"

**The most beautiful-sounding Sanskrit candidate.**

Komala (कोमल) flows like a melody: KOH-mah-lah. It means "soft, tender, beautiful" — describing the app's personality in a single word. No difficult consonant clusters, no pronunciation barriers. Easy to spell, easy to remember. Clean App Store. In Hindi (derived from Sanskrit), "komal" is a common word meaning "soft/gentle," which gives it real-world familiarity for a global audience.

> **Komala** — Screen Wellness & Posture Care

**Pairs with Ocella:** Interesting cross-cultural pair. Latin precision + Sanskrit warmth.  
**Pairs with Restwell:** Similar warmth. Komala is the Eastern philosophy alternative.

---

### 6. Shama (Sanskrit) — "Calm, peace, inner stillness"

**The most serene two-syllable name.**

Shama (शम) means "calm" and "inner peace" — the state the app restores. Just two syllables: SHAH-mah. In Vedantic philosophy, shama is the first of the six virtues (ṣaṭ-sampatti) — the ability to remain calm amid distraction. The App Store has only a food delivery app called "Shama" (completely different category, low risk).

> **Shama** — Eye Break & Posture Reminders

**Pairs with Ocella:** Good complement. Ocella names the subject (eye); Shama names the goal (calm).  
**Pairs with Restwell:** Very similar energy — both communicate rest and calm. Shama has more spiritual depth.

---

### 7. Mridu (Sanskrit) — "Gentle, soft, mild"

**Short, distinctive, unexpected.**

Mridu (मृदु) is the Sanskrit equivalent of "gentle" — the word that defines this app's personality. Two syllables: MREE-doo. The "mr" opening is unusual in English but learnable (like "Mr." without the period). Clean App Store. The word is used in Ayurvedic medicine to describe a gentle, soothing quality — the opposite of harsh or aggressive.

> **Mridu** — Gentle Eye & Posture Breaks

**Pairs with Ocella:** Interesting contrast — Latin elegance vs. Sanskrit earthiness.  
**Pairs with Restwell:** Different tier. Mridu is more niche; Restwell is more mainstream.

---

### 8. Ikshana (Sanskrit) — "Seeing, beholding, observation"

**The Sanskrit word for conscious seeing.**

Ikshana (इक्षण) goes beyond just "sight" — it means "the act of seeing with awareness," beholding rather than merely looking. Three syllables: IK-shah-nah. The only App Store presence is an Android-only neuro-vision app (no iOS conflict). The word connects eye care to mindfulness: the app teaches you to be conscious of how you use your eyes.

> **Ikshana** — Screen Break & Posture Care

**Pairs with Ocella:** Both eye-focused. Ocella (Latin "little eye") + Ikshana (Sanskrit "conscious seeing").  
**Pairs with Restwell:** Different lane. Ikshana emphasizes vision; Restwell emphasizes rest.

---

### 9. Tasis (Greek) — "Stretching, extension"

**Clean, short, strong.**

Tasis (τάσις) means "stretching" or "extension" — capturing the posture/stretch dimension of the app. Two syllables: TAH-sis. The App Store has only TASIS school parent apps (completely different, very low confusion). Root of English "tension" but without the negative connotation — tasis is the *healthy* stretch. Works well for an app that reminds you to stretch and straighten.

> **Tasis** — Eye & Posture Break Reminders

**Pairs with Ocella:** Complementary. Ocella = eyes, Tasis = body. Together they cover both features.  
**Pairs with Restwell:** Different angle. Tasis is active (stretching); Restwell is passive (resting).

---

### 10. Obtutus (Latin) — "Fixed gaze, steady stare"

**For the user who knows their gaze is the problem.**

Obtutus means "a fixed, unmoving gaze" — literally the unhealthy behavior the app corrects. It's self-aware: naming the problem to offer the solution. Three syllables: ob-TOO-tus. Completely clean App Store. Strong, distinctive, impossible to confuse with anything else. More intellectual/literary than warm, but highly brandable for the right audience.

> **Obtutus** — Break Your Stare, Rest Your Eyes

**Pairs with Ocella:** Both Latin, both eye-focused. Obtutus names the problem; Ocella names what it protects.  
**Pairs with Restwell:** Contrasting energy. Obtutus is clinical/smart; Restwell is warm/approachable.

## Notes for Team

- **For Yashas:** If Ocella is your #1, the best *complement* from this round is **Anesis** (relief) or **Mollis** (soft) — both are Latin/Greek classical words at the same tier of elegance. If you want to stay in Sanskrit to match the yin-yang aesthetic, **Nimesha** (blink/moment) is the strongest new find. **Komala** (soft/tender) is the most beautiful-sounding Sanskrit option.
- **For Danny (Design):** Anesis (flowing, water-like) and Komala (melodic, warm) have the strongest aesthetic resonance with the sage-green yin-yang design. Mollis pairs beautifully with soft visual design elements.
- **For Frank (Legal):** All Top 10 new candidates have clean or very-low-risk App Store landscapes. Formal trademark search recommended on any that advance.

---

## Key Insight

The most interesting pattern across this round: **the "gentle/soft" concept produces the best app names across all three languages** — Mollis (Latin), Komala (Sanskrit), and Mridu (Sanskrit) are all strong. The app's core personality is gentleness, and these words let the name carry that personality directly. Meanwhile, **Anesis** (Greek, "relief") is the single best new find because it captures *what the app delivers* in a word that sounds as soothing as its meaning.
